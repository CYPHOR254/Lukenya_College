School of Engineering

The School of Engineering and Technical Studies was established in 2014 and is one of the fastest-growing departments at Lukenya College. The department offers Artisan, Craft, and Diploma programs designed to equip learners with practical skills, technical expertise, and entrepreneurial competencies aligned with industry demands.

Department Overview

Over the years, the department has grown from fewer than 20 students to over 150, graduating more than 300 skilled professionals who are actively contributing to various sectors of the economy.

Our programmes are competency-based and structured to combine classroom instruction with practical training, industrial exposure, and project development.

Assessment & Certification

Assessment includes:

  • Continuous Assessment Tests (CATs)
  • End-of-term examinations
  • Trade projects
  • National examinations

All national examinations are administered by the Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC).

Progression between modules is subject to successful completion of the preceding module.

Diploma Programmes Offered

  • The Department currently offers the following diploma programs:
  • Diploma in Building Technology
  • Diploma in Civil Engineering
  • Diploma in Electrical and Electronics Engineering (Power Option)
  • Diploma in Food and Beverage Production, Sales & Service
  • Diploma in Social Work and Community Development
  • Diploma in General Agriculture

Each program integrates technical theory, practical training, entrepreneurship education, and project development to prepare graduates for employment, self-employment, and further studies.

Training Approach

  • Our training model emphasizes:
  • Practical skills development
  • Technical drawing and design
  • Entrepreneurship and business planning
  • Project-based learning
  • Industrial exposure

Students undertake trade projects and business plan development in their final module under close academic supervision.

Industrial Attachment

All students complete a minimum of 330 hours of industrial attachment in relevant industries. This hands-on exposure enables trainees to apply classroom knowledge in real work environments and gain valuable professional experience.
